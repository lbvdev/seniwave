What is a CMS, really?

Content Management System is the backstage engine of your product: authoring, structure, permissions, media, and integrations — without tapping engineering for every change. For content‑heavy platforms, the CMS is mission control.

  • Dashboard with scheduling, filters, and status tracking
  • Visual editor for text, images, video, embeds
  • Custom content types with tailored fields
  • Media library & asset reuse
  • Role‑based access (writers, editors, admins)
  • SEO & Open Graph tools, sitemaps
  • Localization for multi‑language content
  • Analytics hooks & performance insights
  • CRM‑lite: leads, forms, newsletter capture

Note: "Custom CMS" refers to a system tailored to your workflow. It can be built monolithic (backend+frontend coupled) or headless (content via API to any frontend).

Monolithic (e.g., WordPress)

Backend, frontend, and templating live together. Fast to launch, opinionated, plugin-rich. Great for classic sites and content teams who want speed.

Headless (Sanity, Strapi, Directus, or WordPress Headless)

CMS manages content and serves it via API (REST/GraphQL). Any frontend (web/app/kiosk) consumes it. Flexible, scalable, omnichannel.

Custom CMS (Monolithic or Headless)

Tailored UX, data model, and permissions. Built with Django/Flask/Node/Rails. Exact workflows, no plugin bloat, scale-ready — either coupled or headless.

Which one should you choose?

Choose Monolithic if you need a blog, portfolio, or straightforward business site, your budget is tight with a 2–6 week timeline, and you're comfortable working with themes and a plugin ecosystem.

Choose Headless if you need omni-channel distribution across web, app, or kiosk, performance and scalability are non-negotiable, and your developers prefer modern stacks and CI/CD.

Choose a Custom CMS if you've outgrown templates and plugin glue, you need exact workflows and permissions with long-term ownership, and you're building a product-led platform with a multi-year roadmap.
